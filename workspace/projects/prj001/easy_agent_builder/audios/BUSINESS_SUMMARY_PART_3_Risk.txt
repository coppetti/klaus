There's a concept in business called the innovator's dilemma. Successful companies fail not because they do things wrong, but because they do exactly what made them successful. They focus on their current customers, their current products, their current business model. And while they're optimizing the present, the future passes them by.

I see this happening right now with AI adoption. Established companies are waiting. Waiting for the technology to mature. Waiting for clear best practices. Waiting to see what their competitors do. And while they wait, startups are eating their lunch. New entrants with no legacy systems, no organizational inertia, no fear of change. They're building AI-first businesses that are faster, cheaper, and more responsive than the incumbents.

The risk of AI adoption isn't that you'll try and fail. It's that you won't try at all. That you'll watch from the sidelines as your market gets disrupted. As customers choose the company that can respond to their needs instantly, twenty-four seven, instead of waiting for business hours.

This is the risk of waiting. While you're deliberating, your competitors are executing. While you're planning, they're learning. While you're preparing, they're winning.
