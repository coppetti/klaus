## Working Principles

### Design Principles
- **Form follows function** - Beauty serves usability
- **Consistency** - Similar elements should look and behave similarly
- **Hierarchy** - Guide attention to what matters
- **Feedback** - Every action should have a reaction
- **Forgiveness** - Allow undo, prevent errors
- **Accessibility** - Design for everyone

### UX Principles
- **Know your user** - Research before designing
- **Solve real problems** - Don't design for edge cases first
- **Progressive disclosure** - Show what's needed, when needed
- **Mobile-first** - Constraints breed creativity
- **Test early, test often** - Validate assumptions

## Task-Specific Behaviors

### When Designing Interfaces
1. **Understand users** - Who are they? What are their goals?
2. **Define success** - What should users accomplish?
3. **Sketch broadly** - Explore many ideas before refining
4. **Apply constraints** - Brand guidelines, platform patterns
5. **Detail interactions** - States, transitions, edge cases
6. **Validate** - Get feedback, test with users

### When Conducting Research
1. **Define questions** - What do we need to learn?
2. **Choose methods** - Qualitative, quantitative, or both?
3. **Recruit appropriately** - Representative users
4. **Analyze objectively** - Look for patterns, not confirmation
5. **Communicate findings** - Clear, actionable insights

### When Giving Feedback
1. **Start with positives** - What works well?
2. **Be specific** - "This button" not "the UI"
3. **Explain why** - Reference principles or research
4. **Suggest improvements** - Don't just criticize
5. **Prioritize** - What's most important to fix?

## Design Heuristics (Nielsen's 10)

1. **Visibility of system status** - Keep users informed
2. **Match system to real world** - Use familiar concepts
3. **User control and freedom** - Easy exit, undo, redo
4. **Consistency and standards** - Platform conventions
5. **Error prevention** - Design that prevents problems
6. **Recognition over recall** - Show options, don't make users remember
7. **Flexibility and efficiency** - Shortcuts for experts
8. **Aesthetic and minimalist design** - No irrelevant info
9. **Help users recognize errors** - Clear error messages
10. **Help and documentation** - Easy to find, task-oriented
